🎯 What It Does

The Accumulation/Distribution scanner detects institutional smart money buying patterns by combining five volume-based indicators that reveal hidden accumulation before major price moves.

💡 Key Concept: "Accumulation" means large institutions are quietly buying shares while the price consolidates. This typically happens before major breakouts, giving an early entry advantage.

📈 Core Indicators

1. A/D Line (Accumulation/Distribution Line) — slope analysis over AD_LINE_PERIOD = 20 bars with linear regression.
Formula: ((Close - Low) - (High - Close)) / (High - Low) × Volume, cumulative.
2. OBV (On-Balance Volume) — 20-bar regression slope; used to confirm A/D alignment.
3. CMF (Chaikin Money Flow) — CMF_PERIOD = 20. Ideal range: -0.05 to +0.15.
4. Bullish Divergence — price making lower lows while A/D makes higher lows over MIN_DIVERGENCE_DAYS = 10.
5. Volume Bias Ratio — up-volume / down-volume. > 1.3 = accumulation, < 0.7 = distribution.

✅ Entry Criteria (ALL must pass)

  • Bullish accumulation signal (distribution side disabled)
  • A/D Line trending up: slope > 0 with R² > 0.5
  • CMF > CMF_MINIMUM = -0.2
  • Accumulation score ≥ 4 from five components (A/D trend 2pt, OBV 2pt, CMF>0.1 1pt, divergence 2pt, volume bias 1pt)
  • Quality score ≥ ACCUMULATION_THRESHOLD = 80
  • Avg dollar volume ≥ MIN_AVG_DOLLAR_VOLUME ($5M test / $50M production)
  • Sector NOT in BAD_SECTORS (Utilities, Real Estate, Consumer Defensive)

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: 3-7% below entry or below recent swing low
  • Position Size: Quality ≥90 = 3%, 80-89 = 2%, <80 not taken
  • Holding Period: 2-8 weeks (pattern requires time to resolve)

📝 Summary

Composite of 5 volume-based indicators. Quality ≥80 required. Production requires divergence + OBV alignment; test mode relaxes these. Best signals: neutral volume (0.9-1.2 ratio) with slight-positive CMF and strong A/D trend.
