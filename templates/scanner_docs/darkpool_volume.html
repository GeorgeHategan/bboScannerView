🎯 What It Does

Detects institutional dark pool volume spikes — unusual bursts of off-exchange trading that signal hidden accumulation or distribution before the move becomes visible on the regular tape.

💡 Key Concept: Dark pools are private venues where large institutions trade without showing their intent on the public order book. When dark pool volume suddenly spikes 5-10× normal with a bullish bias and high dollar premium, an institution is almost certainly building a position. The retail market typically lags this signal by 1-3 days.

📡 Data Source

Reads pre-aggregated signals from the MotherDuck table options_data.darkpool_signals. Fields include date, ticker, direction, dp_volume, dp_premium, buy_sell_ratio, avg_block_size, bullish_pct. The upstream pipeline aggregates TAQ and FINRA dark pool prints into these daily signals; the scanner just filters and scores.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Volume Ratio (20-Day Rolling)

Today's dark pool volume vs the 20-day rolling average of dark pool volume for that symbol. MIN_VOLUME_RATIO = 5.0× is the floor for consideration; EXTREME_RATIO = 10.0× flips the signal type from STRONG to EXTREME.

Formula: today_dp_volume / mean(last_20d_dp_volume)

Impact: Up to 40 points of the total 100. This is the single biggest component because volume anomalies are the clearest institutional footprint.

2. Dollar Premium

Total dollar value of dark pool trades on the signal day. Must exceed MIN_PREMIUM = $1,000,000. This filter distinguishes real institutional flow from retail-sized or algo-fragmented prints.

Logic: A $10M premium signal carries institutional conviction; a $500k signal is noise in large-cap stocks.

3. Bullish Percentage / Direction

Percent of the day's dark pool prints executed near the ask (bullish / buying) vs near the bid (bearish / selling). Minimum MIN_BULLISH_PCT = 60% OR explicit direction = BULLISH classification from the upstream pipeline.

Impact: 95%+ bullish scores 20 pts; 80%+ scores 15; 60%+ scores 10.

4. Average Block Size

Avg shares per print on the signal day. Institutions often execute in large blocks (≥ 10,000 shares per print); the larger the block, the stronger the conviction the signal represents.

Tiers: 50k+ = 10 pts, 20k+ = 7, 10k+ = 5, < 10k = 2.

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: 3-5% below signal day low
  • Position Size: Smaller than technical setups — dark pool signals lead but can precede the move by days to weeks
  • Holding Period: 1-4 weeks
  • Combine with: A technical entry trigger from another scanner for precise timing — dark pool is the conviction overlay, not the entry signal itself

📝 Summary

Four-component scoring on pre-aggregated dark pool data: volume ratio (40), premium (30), bullish bias (20), block size (10). Best used in combination — standalone dark pool signals are noisy, but a dark pool extreme co-occurring with a technical breakout is one of the highest-conviction setups in the suite.
