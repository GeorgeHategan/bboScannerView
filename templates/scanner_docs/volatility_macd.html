🎯 What It Does

A volatility-weighted MACD variant that produces three distinct signal types — expansion_crossover, compression_coiling, and momentum_confirmed — each capturing a different phase of the volatility-momentum cycle.

💡 Key Concept: Classic MACD ignores volatility — it fires the same way in quiet and chaotic markets. V-MACD weights the momentum reading by ATR%, so a MACD flip during rising volatility is flagged differently than a flip during compression. This separates explosive setups from coiling ones.

📈 Core Concepts

1. V-MACD Calculation

Built on a standard 12/26/9 EMA structure, but scaled by ATR(14) so that readings are comparable across stocks of different volatility profiles.

Formula: V-MACD = EMA(12) − EMA(26), with histogram = V-MACD − signal_line, signal_line = EMA(9) of V-MACD.

2. Signal Type A — Expansion Crossover

V-MACD crosses above its signal line while ATR% is rising and V-MACD > 0.1. This is the "explosive breakout" mode — momentum flipping bullish at the same moment volatility is expanding.

Criteria: crossover = True AND atr_rising = True AND v_macd > 0.1.

3. Signal Type B — Compression Coiling

V-MACD near zero (|value| < 0.3), histogram flat (|value| < 0.1), ATR% low (< 2.0%). This is the watchlist mode — stock is coiled, but no directional signal yet. Use these for anticipation, not immediate entry.

Logic: Compressed V-MACD with flat histogram = dormant energy. When volatility starts rising, these stocks often flip to expansion-crossover signals — catching them early via coiling gives advance notice.

4. Signal Type C — Momentum Confirmed

V-MACD already above signal line, histogram expanding, ATR rising, and volume ≥ 1.3×. This captures trends that are already in motion with institutional participation — lower R:R than expansion crossovers but higher win rate.

Criteria: v_macd > signal AND histogram_expanding AND atr_rising AND rvol ≥ 1.3.

5. ATR% Dual Role (Rising vs Low)

ATR% plays opposite roles depending on the signal type. For expansion/confirmed, rising ATR is good (higher scores at 4%+). For coiling, low ATR is good (higher scores below 1.5%). Same metric, inverted grading.

6. Price Structure Overlay

Stock must sit above both SMA50 and SMA20, with distance from SMA20 ≥ 2%. This prevents the scanner from firing V-MACD signals in downtrending charts where the math works but the trade doesn't.

✅ Entry Criteria (any signal type)

  • Meets one of the three signal-type criteria above
  • Price above SMA 20
  • Stock price $5 ≤ price ≤ $500
  • Avg dollar volume ≥ $50,000,000
  • Min signal strength ≥ 60

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below recent swing low or SMA 20, typically 4-6% below entry
  • Signal Type Differences:
    • Expansion: largest positions (highest R:R)
    • Confirmed: medium positions (already moving)
    • Coiling: watchlist only — no entry until a resolution signal fires
  • Holding Period: 1-3 weeks typical

📝 Summary

Five-component scoring with three distinct signal types on a volatility-adjusted MACD. The triple-mode design is the differentiator — coiling signals pre-position the trader, expansion signals are entries, and confirmed signals are add-ons to existing positions.
