🎯 What It Does

Combines technical swing-low support with options put wall positioning to identify stocks trading just above a double-reinforced floor. The thesis: when a technical support level lines up with a heavy put-wall strike below, that level tends to hold hard.

💡 Key Concept: Market-maker put walls create a "gamma wall" that reluctantly gets pushed through — dealers must buy stock to hedge if price approaches the strike, providing mechanical demand. When that same level also shows multiple prior swing-low touches, you have both a psychological floor (technical) and a mechanical floor (options flow).

📈 Core Concepts

1. Swing-Low Support Clustering

The scanner scans the last 60 days for swing lows and clusters them by price within support_tolerance = 3.0%. A level with ≥ 2 touches, separated by at least 3 trading days, qualifies as support.

Logic: More touches + longer time spread = stronger memory. Levels defended across 30+ days score higher than levels defended twice this week.

2. Put Wall Open Interest

Reads today's options flow from the options_data database. A qualifying put wall has OI ≥ 5,000 contracts (min_put_wall_oi) at a strike below current price, within max_put_wall_distance = 15% of price.

Scoring tiers: 100k+ OI = 15 pts; 50k+ = 12; 20k+ = 9; 10k+ = 6; 5k+ = 3.

3. Stacked Put Walls (Primary + Secondary + Tertiary)

Beyond the largest put wall, the scanner also credits secondary and tertiary walls. Multiple walls stacked below price create a "put wall staircase" — price has to break through several dealer hedges to go lower.

Bonus: Secondary ≥ 20k = 5 pts, ≥ 10k = 3. Tertiary ≥ 20k = 5, ≥ 10k = 2.

4. Wall-to-Support Alignment

Highest-quality signals have the put wall strike landing right at technical support. The scanner scores alignment: wall strike within -2% to +5% of support = 10 pts; within 10% = 6 pts; within 15% = 3 pts.

Logic: Technical support alone is defensible but can break. Options support alone can shift as walls roll. When both point to the same price, the level has the highest probability of holding.

5. Entry Position (Above Support, Not Below)

Price must be ≥ support level and within max_distance_to_support = 8% above it. Below support = the level has broken, disqualify. Above 8% = stop would be too wide for reasonable position sizing.

Scoring: Within 2% above = 10 pts; within 4% = 8; within 6% = 5; within 8% = 3.

6. Put/Call Ratio Context

An elevated put/call ratio (≥ 1.0) indicates bearish positioning — but contrarians see this as "puts to eat" above support, setting up gamma squeeze conditions when price bounces.

Bonus: P/C ≥ 1.0 = 5 pts; ≥ 0.5 = 3 pts.

✅ Entry Criteria

  • ≥ 2 swing lows within support_tolerance = 3%
  • Put wall OI ≥ min_put_wall_oi = 5,000
  • Price within 8% above support (NOT below)
  • Put wall strike below current price
  • Put wall strike within 15% below price
  • Signal strength ≥ MIN_SIGNAL_STRENGTH = 50
  • Stock price $5 ≤ price ≤ $500
  • Avg volume ≥ 100,000

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below the put wall strike (not below support — the wall is the deeper floor)
  • Target: Prior swing high or R:R ≥ 2:1
  • Position Size: 1-2% risk — tighter stops enable larger positions
  • Holding Period: 2-4 weeks typical
  • Exit Rule: Abort if the put wall materially shifts (OI drains or strike moves lower)

📝 Summary

Three-group weighted score (support 40 + put wall 40 + position 20) combining technical swing-lows with options flow data. Requires integration with the options_data database for put wall OI. Best used on mid-to-large-cap names where options flow is meaningful.
