🎯 What It Does

Detects Golden Cross events — when a fast moving average crosses above a slow one, signaling a potential long-term trend change. Scored across six components to distinguish genuine regime changes from low-quality crossovers.

⚠️ Important: This scanner uses 20-day MA crossing above 200-day MA — not the textbook 50/200. The 20-day crossover is faster and more actionable, but fires more often and therefore requires the quality filters below to separate real regime changes from noise.

📈 Core Concepts

1. 20/200 Crossover Detection

Looks for a fresh crossover within the last 5 trading days (cross_lookback_days = 5). The 20 DMA must close above the 200 DMA after having been below it; the cross is then marked on the exact day it happened.

Logic: Freshness matters — day-of-cross entries score 30 pts, 5-day-old crosses score only 10. After 10+ days the setup has usually already moved.

2. Flat 200 DMA Filter (Regime Change vs Acceleration)

The 200 DMA slope must be < max_200ma_slope = 5.0%. This is the single most important quality filter — it distinguishes genuine trend reversals from mere accelerations of an existing uptrend.

Logic: If the 200 DMA is already trending up sharply, a 20/200 cross is just a late reaction to an existing trend, not a regime change. Flat 200s produce the most durable post-cross trends.

Formula: (sma_200[today] - sma_200[20d ago]) / sma_200[20d ago] < 5%

3. Cross-Day Volume Confirmation

Volume on the cross day itself should be ≥ 1.5× average. Cross-day volume ratio is scored independently from the volume trend (next concept).

Impact: Up to 10 pts. 3× volume on the cross day = 10 pts; 1.2× = 4 pts.

4. Price-vs-200-at-Cross (40% vs 18% Success)

A powerful statistical filter: crosses where price was above the 200 SMA at the moment of cross historically succeed at ~40%; crosses where price was below succeed at only ~18%.

Impact: Price ≥ 8% above 200 at cross = 15 pts; > 5% = 13; > 3% = 11; > 1% = 8; just above = 5.

5. MA Separation (Not Too Tight, Not Too Wide)

Separation between 20 DMA and 200 DMA is scored best in the 1.5-4% range. Too tight = marginal cross that may re-cross back; too wide = stock has already run and cross is late.

6. Volume Trend (Accumulation Leading In)

Compares recent volume to historical volume. A rising trend (+50%+) signals institutions were already accumulating into the cross = 10 pts.

✅ Entry Criteria

  • 20 DMA crosses above 200 DMA within last 5 days
  • 200 DMA slope < max_200ma_slope = 5.0%
  • Price currently above BOTH 20 DMA and 200 DMA
  • Price was ABOVE 200 SMA at the time of cross
  • Volume on cross day ≥ 1.5× average
  • RSI ≥ 50 at cross (momentum present)
  • Avg volume ≥ min_volume = 100,000
  • Signal strength ≥ min_signal_strength = 60

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below 200-day SMA
  • Target: 20-50%+ over 6-12 months
  • Holding Period: 6-12 months (long-term trend)
  • Position Size: 2-5% (larger due to high historical quality)

📝 Summary

Six-component score with a hard floor of 60. Uses 20/200 (not classic 50/200) for faster signals, offset by strict quality filters — especially the 5% max 200 DMA slope, which is the scanner's signature "regime change" filter.
