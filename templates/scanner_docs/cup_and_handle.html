🎯 What It Does

Detects the classic Cup and Handle pattern — a rounded cup-shaped base followed by a shallow handle pullback, then a breakout above the right rim. One of the most durable chart patterns in the William O'Neil / IBD methodology.

💡 Key Concept: The cup is a multi-week reaccumulation base where weak holders exit near the left rim and strong holders rebuild positions at the lows. The handle is a final shakeout before institutions push price above the right rim. Shape matters: a clean "U" shape with tight handle beats a sharp "V" every time.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Cup Formation (Phase 1)

The scanner finds three anchor points: the left rim (prior high), the cup low (bottom), and the right rim (recovery high). Duration: 20-60 days. Depth: 10-30% from rim to low.

Logic: Cups shallower than 10% don't represent real accumulation; cups deeper than 30% indicate broken trends, not bases.

2. Cup Quality — U-Shape Verification (Phase 2)

A valid cup is U-shaped, not V-shaped. The scanner measures the variance of daily percent changes on each side of the low. Both halves must have variance < variance_max_pct = 3% per side.

Formula: std(pct_change(left_half)) < 3% AND std(pct_change(right_half)) < 3%

Symmetry: The right rim must recover to at least right_rim_min = 80% of left rim height (relaxed from 90% for signal density).

3. Handle Formation (Phase 3)

After the right rim forms, the stock pulls back slightly to form the handle. Duration: 5-20 days. Depth: 3-15% of the right rim. Ideally the handle forms on lighter volume than the cup itself.

Logic: The handle is the final weak-hand shakeout. Too shallow (< 3%) = no shakeout happened. Too deep (> 15%) = new downtrend, not a pause.

4. Breakout Proximity (Phase 4)

Current price near the handle high with RVOL ≥ min_rvol = 0.8 (relaxed from 1.0). Pattern must be ≤ 10 days old — older patterns frequently resolve before they can be acted on.

Impact: Proximity scoring gives up to 10 pts — the closer to breakout, the better the R:R.

✅ Entry Criteria

  • Quality score ≥ quality_threshold = 50 (relaxed from 65 for signal density)
  • Right rim ≥ 80% of left rim height
  • RVOL ≥ min_rvol = 0.8
  • Cup depth 10-30%, duration 20-60 days
  • Handle depth 3-15%, duration 5-20 days

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below handle low (typically 3-5% below entry)
  • Target: Cup depth projected up from breakout (measured move)
  • Position Size: 90+=3%, 80-89=2%, 70-79=1%
  • Holding Period: 4-12 weeks

📝 Summary

Four-phase structural detection with 5-component quality scoring. Thresholds intentionally relaxed (quality 50+, rvol 0.8+, right rim 80%+) for signal density. The U-shape variance check is what distinguishes clean cups from V-bottoms.
