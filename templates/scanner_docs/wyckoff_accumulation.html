🎯 What It Does

Detects stocks in Wyckoff Phase C / D accumulation: sitting in a tight trading range just under resistance, with a recent Sign of Strength (SOS) bar and price positioned in the top of the range, poised for breakout. Calibrated on 761 real Wyckoff-style winners from Nov 2024 – Feb 2026.

💡 Key Concept: The textbook Wyckoff narrative includes a "spring" (false breakdown below support) as the central tell. Backtest shows springs do NOT discriminate — 29% of winners had them vs 31% of non-winners. What DOES discriminate: position in range (winners sit in the top 20%), a properly-defined SOS bar (gain + volume + upper-range close combined), and trend extension above the 200-day. v2 drops the noise and keeps only what works.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Multi-Window Trading Range Detection

The scanner tries 20, 30, 45, and 60-day windows and picks the tightest qualifying range. A range qualifies when width is between 6% and 18%, AND at least 60% of bars close inside [support × 0.98, resistance × 1.02].

Why multi-window: v1's single 30-day lookback missed valid 45-day bases that were tight, and flagged wider 30-day chop that wasn't really a base.

2. Position in Range ≥ 80% (NEW — top discriminator)

Measures where the current close sits from support (0%) to resistance (100%). Winners cluster in the top 20% of their ranges. Non-winners are spread evenly through the range.

Logic: A stock trading at the bottom of a base isn't about to break out — it's about to retest support. Only setups already pushing against the lid deserve a signal.

3. Sign of Strength Bar (Redefined)

An SOS bar is a single-day event in the last 20 days with ALL of: ≥ 2.5% gain, volume ≥ 1.5× the prior 50-day average, AND close in the upper 60% of the bar's range.

Why the upper-close requirement matters: a 3% up bar that closes mid-range on high volume is supply absorption — not demand. Only bars that close strong count.

4. Trend Extension Above 200 DMA

Price must be ≥ 5% above the 200-day moving average. Below that, there's no confirmed uptrend — you're looking at a dead-cat consolidation, not an accumulation base.

5. Within 10% of 52-Week High

As with volatility_squeeze and bull_flag, proximity to the 52-week high is a strong universal discriminator. Setups far from the prior high are typically rallying within a downtrend, not breaking into new territory.

6. RSI ≤ 78 (no "cool RSI" preference)

Real winner median RSI is ~65-70. v1 rewarded cooler RSI (40-55 for springs) — the backtest shows this was inverted. v2 simply caps overextension at RSI 78; no bonus for being cool.

✅ Entry Criteria (ALL 11 hard gates)

  1. Avg dollar volume 20d ≥ $5,000,000
  2. Price ≥ $3
  3. Close > SMA50
  4. Close ≥ 5% above SMA200
  5. Within 10% of 52-week high
  6. Trading range: best-fit of (20, 30, 45, 60)-day windows, width 6-18%, ≥60% bars inside [S·0.98, R·1.02]
  7. Position in range ≥ 80%
  8. Distance to resistance: -1% to 5%
  9. RSI(14) ≤ 78
  10. Sign of Strength bar in last 20 days: ≥2.5% gain, ≥1.5× vol, close in upper 60% of bar
  11. (Ranking) signal_strength ≥ 60

📊 Validation Methodology

  1. Winner definition: Stocks that consolidated in a Wyckoff-style range with an SOS bar inside, then broke out and gained ≥ 15% within 40 days without ≥ 10% drawdown in the following 10 days. Found 761 across the universe from Nov 2024 – Feb 2026.
  2. Feature profiling: Measured 13+ features on winners + 1,000 random non-winner positions. Confirmed that spring, volume contraction, and cool-RSI features do NOT discriminate — and that position-in-range, SOS composition, and trend extension DO.
  3. Grid search: Tested multiple threshold profiles to pick QUALITY (highest SNR) over BROAD (highest recall).
  4. Out-of-sample validation: Trained thresholds on pre-July 2025 winners, applied unchanged to post-July 2025. Thresholds held on unseen data — not overfit.

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: support − 0.5 × ATR(14) — the "if this breaks, the base has failed" price
  • Target: resistance + (resistance − support) — classic Wyckoff measured move
  • Position Size: Quality 90+ = 3%, 80-89 = 2%, 60-79 = 1% account risk
  • Holding Period: 2-8 weeks typical
  • Exit Rule: If price doesn't clear resistance within 10 trading days of the signal, the base is going stale

📝 Summary

v2 keeps the Wyckoff framing but replaces v1's narrative-driven features (spring, volume contraction, sector bonuses, cool RSI) with evidence-driven ones (position in range, multi-window base fit, properly-defined SOS, trend extension). Eleven hard gates. Signal-to-noise ratio improved to 10.14× on out-of-sample data. Result: fewer but much higher-quality Wyckoff setups.
