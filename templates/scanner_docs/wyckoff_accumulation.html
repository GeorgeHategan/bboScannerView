🎯 What It Does

Detects stocks in Wyckoff Phase C / D accumulation — specifically identifying spring actions (false breakdowns below support that reverse) and signs of strength (high-volume thrusts off the lows). Based on Richard Wyckoff's 1930s method for tracking institutional accumulation.

💡 Key Concept: Wyckoff's insight: institutions accumulate in trading ranges, not uptrends. They engineer "springs" — deliberate pushes below support on low volume to shake out weak hands — then confirm strength via high-volume thrust bars ("signs of strength"). This scanner detects both, in order: spring first, then SoS.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Trading Range Detection (Phase B Foundation)

Before anything else, the scanner identifies a trading range: 10-30 days of price action with width ≤ MAX_RANGE_WIDTH = 13% (balanced from 15%). Without a defined range there's no "support" to spring below.

Formula: (range_high - range_low) / range_low ≤ 13%, over 10-30 days.

2. Spring Action (Phase C — The Shakeout)

A "spring" is a false breakdown: price dips SPRING_DEPTH = 2% below range support on low volume (< SPRING_VOLUME_MULT = 1.5×) and recovers back into the range within 3 days.

Logic: Low-volume breakdowns are engineered shakeouts — insiders letting weak hands capitulate at the lows before loading up. The fast recovery confirms there's no real selling pressure below support.

Impact: Spring present = 30 pts, +10 bonus if the spring was recent (≤5 days ago).

3. Sign of Strength (Phase D — Institutional Reveal)

A "Sign of Strength" (SoS) is a 2.5%+ up bar on ≥ SOS_VOLUME_MULT = 1.8× volume with RSI ≥ 55. This is the moment institutions stop hiding — they absorb supply openly and leave a vol+momentum footprint.

Logic: A spring without an SoS is just a false alarm. A spring followed by an SoS is the textbook Wyckoff accumulation completion — the phase C-to-D transition.

Impact: SoS present = 40 pts, +15 bonus if volume ≥ 2.5× average (very high conviction).

4. Volume Profile Contraction (Optional)

Ideally, average volume in the second half of the range is lower than the first half — institutions accumulating quietly as the range matures. This is optional (REQUIRE_VOLUME_CONTRACTION = False for signal density), but contributes up to 20 pts when present.

5. Entry Timing (Reward Early, Penalize Late)

Early entries (> 5% below resistance) score +15 bonus — you're catching the trade right as Phase D begins. Late entries (< 2% below resistance) get a -10 penalty — the move is nearly done and R:R is poor.

6. Sector Context (Historical Performance Bias)

Some sectors have historically produced cleaner Wyckoff patterns (Basic Materials, Technology during bases). Others regularly fail these patterns (Energy, Real Estate during this scanner's history). The sector bonus is ±15 pts based on the historical empirical record.

✅ Entry Criteria

  • Trading range: 10-30 days, width ≤ MAX_RANGE_WIDTH = 13%
  • Spring detected: ≥ 2% below support on < 1.5× volume, recovered within 3 days
  • Sign of Strength: ≥ 2.5% gain on ≥ 1.8× volume with RSI ≥ 55
  • Price at/near support, NOT at resistance
  • Stock price $10 ≤ price ≤ $500 (floor raised from $5)
  • Avg dollar volume ≥ $50,000,000
  • Quality score ≥ 50 (balanced from 70)

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below the spring low — not below support itself. The spring low IS the definitive "if this breaks, the pattern is invalidated" price.
  • Target: Range width projected up from breakout (measured move), or prior resistance
  • Position Size: 1-2% risk; patterns can take time to resolve
  • Holding Period: 4-12 weeks (Wyckoff setups are swing-to-position horizon)

📝 Summary

Six-component scoring on Wyckoff's Phase C / D accumulation, ordered: trading range → spring → sign of strength. Thresholds have been balanced for signal density — stricter originals produced too few signals. Requires patience: the full spring → SoS sequence can unfold over weeks.
