🎯 What It Does

Detects TA-Lib bullish continuation candlestick patterns — formations that signal an existing uptrend will resume after a brief pause. Unlike the reversal scanner, this one requires an established uptrend; it will not fire on sideways or downtrending stocks.

💡 Key Concept: Continuation patterns are the lowest-risk candlestick trades because the trend is already established. You are not betting on a reversal — you are just timing a pullback entry into an already-working trend.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Trend Classification Gate

Before any pattern matching runs, the scanner classifies the stock's trend using SMA 20 and SMA 50 slopes. The four tiers are checked in order — the moment a stock qualifies for a tier, that label is used.

  • strong_uptrend: price > SMA20 AND price > SMA50 AND SMA20 slope > 5% AND SMA50 slope > 2%
  • moderate_uptrend: price > SMA20 AND price > SMA50
  • weak_uptrend: price > SMA20 OR price > SMA50
  • no_uptrend: skipped — continuation patterns are meaningless without a trend to continue

Impact: Strong trend adds +5 score bonus; moderate +3; weak +1; no_uptrend exits early.

2. Curated Continuation Pattern Library (13 patterns)

TA-Lib has dozens of continuation candidates; this scanner uses 13, weighted 5.0 (marginal) to 9.2 (premium). The standout is CDLMATHOLD — a 5-bar Japanese pattern where a long bullish bar is followed by three small consolidation bars and then another bullish bar, encoding a clean pause-and-resume rhythm.

3. Volume Tiering

Volume is graded in four tiers, not binary: 2.0× = +10 pts, 1.5× = +7 pts, 1.2× = +4 pts, 1.0× = +2 pts. This rewards institutional participation without throwing away clean patterns that happen on average volume.

4. Pattern Stacking

If multiple continuation patterns fire on the same bar, the score gets a cluster bonus: +5 for two, +10 for three, +15 for four or more. This is especially common during strong trends where several continuation patterns align simultaneously.

5. Picked-Symbol Filter

Like the reversal scanner, this one runs only on symbols that another scanner has flagged in the last 30 days. It never produces standalone signals — it confirms existing setups.
