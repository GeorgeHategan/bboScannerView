🎯 What It Does

Detects volatility-contraction bases — stocks coiling in a tight, low-volatility range after a prior uptrend, with Bollinger Band width compressed vs its own history. Based on Mark Minervini's VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) methodology.

💡 Key Concept: Tight consolidation is the precursor to explosive moves. When a stock's range contracts and its Bollinger Width hits a multi-month low and the trend structure stays intact — buyers and sellers have reached equilibrium, and the next directional break tends to travel far. It's the classic "tight before loose" market behavior.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Base Range Tightness

Over the last 10-25 days, the high-to-low range must be ≤ MAX_BASE_RANGE_PCT = 10% (relaxed from 8% for signal density). A 10% range on a $100 stock means the stock has barely moved — exactly what we want before a breakout.

Formula: (base_high - base_low) / base_low ≤ 10%

2. ATR% Compression

Average True Range as a % of price must be ≤ 2.0% (relaxed from 1.5%). Low ATR% confirms that daily ranges are also tight, not just the aggregate base.

Logic: Range tightness without ATR tightness is a deception — wide-ranging days that happen to close near each other can still fit in a 10% box. ATR filter eliminates this.

3. Bollinger Band Width Percentile (6-Month History)

Current BBWidth must be in the bottom 30% of its own 6-month distribution (BBW_PERCENTILE_THRESH = 0.30, relaxed from 0.25). This is the statistical version of "tightness vs this stock's own normal."

Impact: Stocks that are tight for themselves score better than stocks that are tight in absolute terms. A stock normally moving 2%/day that compresses to 0.5%/day is a stronger signal than one that's always been at 0.5%.

4. Compression Ratio (40d / 15d Range)

Range over the last 40 days divided by range over the last 15 days. Must be ≥ 1.8× (relaxed from 2.0). Greater ratios mean the recent window is much tighter than the prior window — active compression.

Formula: (max(last_40d) - min(last_40d)) / (max(last_15d) - min(last_15d)) ≥ 1.8

Impact: Up to 20 pts — compression ratio 2.5+ scores full marks, graduated down from there.

5. Trend Structure Gate (MA Alignment)

Price > 50 DMA AND MA stack aligned: 10 DMA ≥ 20 DMA ≥ 50 DMA. Without this filter, consolidation scans catch stocks that are dying — we want bases in uptrends, not distribution zones.

Overextension guard: Price must not be > 10% above 200 DMA (stocks too extended usually continue down into their base rather than up out of it).

6. Market Regime Filter (SPY + VIX)

Only fires when SPY is above its 50 DMA AND VIX is below 25. Tight consolidations work in bullish regimes; in bearish regimes they tend to resolve to the downside.

✅ Entry Criteria

  • Base range ≤ MAX_BASE_RANGE_PCT = 10% over 10-25 days
  • ATR% ≤ 2.0%
  • BBWidth ≤ 30th percentile of 6-month history
  • Compression ratio (40d/15d) ≥ 1.8×
  • Price above 50 DMA; MA stack 10 ≥ 20 ≥ 50
  • Not > 10% above 200 DMA
  • Avg dollar volume ≥ $1,000,000
  • Quality score ≥ 65 (lowered from 70)

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below the base low (reported as logical_stop, typically 3-5% below entry)
  • Target: 20-50%+ over 4-8 weeks (historical VCP success)
  • Position Size: Tight stops enable larger positions (1-2% risk = large % size)
  • Holding Period: Until breakout fires; abandon if base breaks down

📝 Summary

Seven-component VCP-style scoring with market regime gating. Ultra-selective by design — thresholds have been relaxed from tighter originals just to produce a handful of signals. Best used in bullish regimes where tight-before-loose price action is most reliable.
