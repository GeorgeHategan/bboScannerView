🎯 What It Does

The Breakout scanner implements the Kristjan Qullamaggie methodology — detecting stocks breaking above recent highs with confirming volume, a rising moving-average stack, and hourly timing refinement for earlier entries.

💡 Key Concept: True breakouts combine three forces at once: price clearing a known resistance level (20-day high), institutions participating (volume ≥ 1.5× average), and the stock already being in an uptrend (MA stack aligned). Missing any one = false breakout risk.

📈 Core Concepts

1. 20-Day High Breakout

The stock's close must exceed its highest close over the prior lookback_days = 20 trading days. This establishes that the market has decisively repriced the asset above prior resistance.

Logic: A 20-day high represents about one month of price memory — short enough to be actionable, long enough to be meaningful.

2. Volume Confirmation

Breakout day volume must be at least volume_multiplier = 1.5× the recent average. Low-volume breakouts are the classic "fake out" pattern — price pokes above resistance but nobody's participating, and it collapses back.

Formula: today_volume / avg_volume ≥ 1.5

Impact: Volume confirmation dramatically reduces false-breakout rate. Qullamaggie's research: breakouts on > 1.5× volume historically outperform < 1× breakouts by a wide margin.

3. Moving-Average Stack

Three simultaneous MA filters — Close > SMA 10 > SMA 20 — confirm the stock is in a short-term uptrend before the breakout fires. SMA 50 is tracked for context.

Logic: Without the MA stack, any random pop above the 20-day high triggers. The stack filter ensures we're adding to strength, not catching a counter-trend bounce.

4. Hourly Timing Refinement

After the daily signal fires, the scanner replays the last 195 hours (~30 trading days) of hourly data to find the precise hourly bar where the daily breakout first occurred. If the hourly bar's volume is also ≥ 1.2× its hourly average, that timestamp becomes the reported Breakout_Time and Entry_Price.

Edge: Daily-only breakout scans report at next day's open. Hourly refinement lets traders enter 5-6 hours earlier in many cases — significant when breakouts accelerate fast.

✅ Entry Criteria (ALL 5 must be true)

  1. Price breaks above 20-day high (lookback_days = 20)
  2. Volume spike ≥ volume_multiplier = 1.5× average
  3. Close > SMA 10 (above fast MA)
  4. Close > SMA 20 (above slow MA)
  5. SMA 10 > SMA 20 (confirmed uptrend)

📊 Scoring

Binary detection — no signal_strength score. All 5 conditions must be true; if so, it's a signal. This is why breakout results show N/A in the signal-strength distribution tables elsewhere on this site.

📤 Output Columns

Ticker, Date, Close, Previous_High, Breakout_%, Volume, Avg_Volume, Volume_Ratio, SMA_10, SMA_20, Breakout_Time, Entry_Price, Hourly_Signal, scan_date, chart

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: 3-7% below entry, or below the breakout level itself
  • Profit Target: 10-20% for first scale-out
  • Holding Period: 2-7 days (short-term momentum)
  • Position Size: 0.5-1% account risk per trade

📝 Summary

Binary 5-condition breakout with hourly refinement for entry timing. No quality scoring — the presence of a signal is itself the grade, since all five conditions must hold simultaneously. Highly selective: 25 or fewer signals are typical per scan day, which is by design.
