🎯 What It Does

Detects stocks in a quiet base sitting right under a prior high, positioned for a breakout. Replaces the tight_consolidation and vcp scanners (both were calibrated for textbook Minervini / VCP patterns that almost never occur in the current market).

💡 Key Concept: Real breakout winners aren't always tight Minervini coils. They're stocks already extended well above their 200 DMA, sitting within 5% of a 52-week high, with manageable volatility. This scanner is calibrated on actual historical winners, not textbook patterns.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Proximity to 52-Week High (the top discriminator)

Winners sit at median 3.8% below their 52-week high. Non-winners sit at median 13% below. This single feature is the strongest predictor.

Hard gate: within 5% of the 52-week high.

2. Extension Above 200 DMA

Winners are median 9.8% above their 200 DMA. Non-winners are at or below it. This filter excludes recovery plays and stage-1 bases.

Hard gate: ≥ 5% above the 200 DMA.

3. Reasonable ATR Ceiling (NOT sub-2%)

The old scanners required ATR ≤ 2.0% — this rejected 60% of real winners. Real breakout bases typically form at ATR 2-3%. This scanner uses 2.5% — strict enough to filter chaos, loose enough to catch normal setups.

4. Best-Fit Base Detection (10–25 days)

Rather than pick a fixed window, the scanner tries every lookback from 10 to 25 days and selects the one producing the tightest high-low range. The tightest base found must have range ≤ 15% of low.

Logic: Some bases consolidate for two weeks, others for five. Hard-coding a single window misses real patterns.

5. What We Deliberately Do NOT Require

The backtest showed that these textbook requirements inversely correlated with winners, or didn't discriminate at all:

  • Volume dryup — winners and non-winners both have vol_ratio ≈ 0.97
  • Compression ratio (40d/15d) — non-winners actually compress MORE than winners
  • 3+ monotonically decreasing contractions — rejects all real winners
  • SMA200 rising 1%+ in 30 days — many winners emerge from still-flat 200 DMAs

✅ Entry Criteria (ALL 6 hard gates must pass)

  1. Price above SMA 50 (trend)
  2. Within 5% of 52-week high (positioning)
  3. ≥ 5% above 200 DMA (defined uptrend)
  4. ATR(14) / close ≤ 2.5% (manageable volatility)
  5. Best-fit base range (10-25 day window) ≤ 15% of low
  6. Avg dollar volume 20d ≥ $1,000,000 (liquidity)

📊 Validation Methodology

Built on real data, not theory:

  1. Winner definition: Stock had a 30-60 day consolidation (range ≤ 20%), broke out above it on ≥1.3× avg volume, gained ≥15% within 40 trading days, did not drop ≥10% in following 10 days. Found 380 such winners across 255 symbols.
  2. Feature profiling: Measured 8 features on each winner + 2,000 random non-winner positions. Identified 52-week proximity and extension-above-200 as top discriminators.
  3. Grid search: Tested 320 threshold combinations to find best recall + signal-to-noise balance.
  4. Out-of-sample validation: Trained thresholds on pre-July 2025 winners, applied unchanged to post-July 2025. Recall actually improved on test data (24.5% → 32.1%), confirming thresholds are not overfit.

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below base_low (typically 3-5% below entry)
  • Target: Prior resistance or measured move equal to base height
  • Position Size: 1-2% account risk per trade; setups near 52-week high have asymmetric upside
  • Holding Period: 2-8 weeks typical
  • Exit Rule: If price doesn't clear base_high within 10 trading days, setup is stale

📝 Summary

Six hard gates calibrated on 380 real winners vs 2,000 non-winners. Captures stocks in quiet, tight bases right under 52-week highs in defined uptrends — the actual shape of real breakout setups in the current market, not the textbook shape from trading books written 20+ years ago.
