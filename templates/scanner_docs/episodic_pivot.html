🎯 What It Does

Implements the StockBee Episodic Pivot (EP) methodology — detects gap-ups driven by a fresh catalyst on stocks that were "neglected" (tight price base) prior to the gap. Produces a two-tier output: CONFIRMED candidates (trade-ready) and WATCHLIST candidates (further monitoring).

💡 Key Concept: An EP is the moment a neglected stock suddenly matters. The "episode" is a catalyst (earnings beat, FDA approval, guidance raise) that forces the market to reprice. The "pivot" is the technical result: a large gap on huge volume. Tight prior base + high-quality catalyst + institutional volume = the highest-probability swing setup in the book.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Two-Tier Classification

Every candidate is classified as CONFIRMED (all strict filters pass) or WATCHLIST (looser filters). CONFIRMED candidates are traded; WATCHLIST are monitored in case they tighten up the next day.

Logic: Strict real-time filters (10% gap, premarket vol 3×, <24h catalyst) often disqualify candidates that will still work. The watchlist catches them with relaxed thresholds (5% gap, RVOL 2×, 7-day catalyst) for manual review.

2. Base Quality — StockBee's "Neglect" Criterion

The scanner measures the 60-day price range prior to the gap. Tight bases produce the most explosive EPs because there's no overhead supply from recent buyers.

Logic: A stock that did nothing for 60 days has no sellers watching for "breakeven" to exit. When the catalyst hits, there's no supply to absorb the demand → parabolic move.

  • < 20% range: 100 pts (tight base — ideal)
  • 20-35% range: 60 pts (moderate)
  • 35-50% range: 35 pts (wide — less neglected)
  • < 8% total price change: +20 bonus (very neglected)
  • > 20% total price change: -15 penalty (already trending)

4. Early Volume (Premarket + First 15/20 Min)

Gap size alone can be misleading — gaps without volume close. The scanner checks FOUR volume signals and accepts any one:

  • Premarket volume ≥ 3.0× (vs premarket norm)
  • First 15 min volume ≥ 0.75× (of typical hourly)
  • First 20 min volume ≥ 1.0×
  • Fallback RVOL ≥ 3.0× (vs 50-day average)

Logic: Institutions accumulate in premarket and the first 30 minutes. Strong early volume = institutional participation = move has legs.

5. Earnings Growth Factor

EPs with strong underlying growth outperform. EPS growth YoY maps to a score with a revenue-growth bonus. A 50%+ EPS beat with 30%+ revenue growth scores 115; a negative-EPS gap scores only 25.

6. Market Regime Filter (Qullamaggie QQQ Filter)

EPs fire best in bullish markets. The scanner classifies the market regime using QQQ 10 EMA vs 20 EMA:

  • Bullish: 10 EMA > 20 EMA AND both rising — green light, normal sizing
  • Bearish: 10 EMA < 20 EMA — reduce size or skip
  • Neutral/Unknown: proceed with caution

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below gap-up low or prior day close (typically 3-7%)
  • Position Size: Scale with EP score — maximum only if CONFIRMED + bullish regime + score > 80
  • Holding Period: 1-4 weeks (momentum fades quickly)
  • Exit Rule: Scale out after +15-20%; trail stop on remainder

📝 Summary

Eight-component weighted composite fused with a two-tier CONFIRMED/WATCHLIST classifier, plus a regime filter overlay. The most complex scanner in the suite. Production use requires an integrated news/catalyst feed.
