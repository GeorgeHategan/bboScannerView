🎯 What It Does

Detects the classic bull flag pattern: a strong prior rally (flagpole) followed by a tight shallow pullback (flag), then an imminent breakout. Calibrated on 403 real bull-flag winners from Nov 2024 – Feb 2026.

💡 Key Concept: A real bull flag is not a "wedge compression" pattern. It's a short, shallow pullback near a 52-week high after a strong rally. The old scanner required wedge compression and volume dryup — the backtest showed neither discriminated real winners from losers. This scanner drops those false signals and keeps only what actually works.

📈 Core Concepts

1. Flagpole (the uptrend)

A rally of ≥ 20% over 5-15 days, ending 5-30 days before the scan. The scanner tries every window in that range and keeps the largest gain found.

Why this matters: Without a flagpole, a pullback isn't a flag — it's just a sideways chop. Real bull flags need the prior thrust to set up the release.

2. Flag (the consolidation)

Duration 4-18 days, range ≤ 15% of flag low. Winner median flag length is 8 days — half of non-winner median (19 days). Real flags are short; longer consolidations become full bases or trend reversals.

3. Pullback Depth ≤ 10% (NEW — top discriminator)

Measures drop from the flagpole peak to the flag low. Winners pull back a median of 5.6%; non-winners pull back 11.9%.

Logic: A shallow pullback = holders aren't selling = buyers are still in control. A 15%+ pullback means the rally is breaking down, not just resting.

4. Within 10% of 52-Week High (NEW)

Winners sit at median 4.6% below 52-week high. Non-winners at 14.7%. A setup far from its prior high isn't breaking out — it's just rallying within a downtrend.

5. Strict MA Alignment

Price must be above EMA21, and EMA9 must be above EMA21. This combination was present in 98%+ of winners but only 60-70% of non-winners. The single strongest binary classifier in the data.

6. RSI ≤ 80 (NOT "cool RSI")

The old scanner rewarded RSI 40-65 as a "sweet spot." This was WRONG: real winner median RSI is 68, non-winner median is 54. The hot, extended setups ARE the ones that break out. The new gate simply prevents full overextension (RSI > 80); there's no bonus for cool readings.

✅ Entry Criteria (ALL 10 hard gates)

  1. Flagpole ≥ 20% over 5-15 days, ending 5-30 days ago
  2. Flag length 4-18 bars
  3. Flag range ≤ 15%
  4. Pullback ≤ 10% from pole peak to flag low
  5. Distance to breakout ≤ 5%
  6. Within 10% of 52-week high
  7. Price > EMA21
  8. EMA9 > EMA21
  9. RSI(14) ≤ 80
  10. Avg dollar volume 20d ≥ $1,000,000
  11. (Ranking) signal_strength ≥ 60

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Stop Loss: Below flag_low, buffered by 1×ATR (typical 3-6% below entry)
  • Target: Measured move = flag_high + flagpole height
  • Position Size: Quality 90+=3%, 80-89=2%, 60-79=1% account risk
  • Holding Period: 1-6 weeks typical
  • Exit Rule: If price doesn't clear flag_high within 10 trading days, pattern has failed

📝 Summary

v2 replaces the v1 "wedge" scanner with thresholds calibrated on 403 real winners. Ten hard gates anchored in positional features (flagpole, shallow pullback, 52-week proximity, trend alignment). Wedge compression, volume dryup, and RSI sweet-spot — all classic textbook features — were tested and removed because they did not discriminate. Result: fewer but much higher-quality signals.
